Iñigo Valenzuela Lombera

Iñigo is a coterminal master's student in the Applied Physics department, and earned his BS in Physics here at Stanford in 2021. Iñigo has very broad research interests. For his undergraduate thesis, he worked with Prof. Benjamin Lev on mapping the phase diagram of a material made up of ultracold atoms inside an optical cavity, hoping to find an exotic phase of matter called the spin glass phase. In our group, Iñigo is working on mapping magnetic fields in external spiral galaxies, to better understand the role of magnetic fields in galactic dynamics. Iñigo is originally from Mexico City. Outside of physics, he loves alpinism, winter sports, rock climbing and anything outdoorsy.
